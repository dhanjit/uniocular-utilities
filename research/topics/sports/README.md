# Topic: Sports and physical activity

## Summary

Fast-moving-ball sports are the hardest, but participation — even at elite
level — is achievable with adaptation. Eye protection is non-negotiable.

## Key findings

- Tracking fast objects is the core challenge (tennis, squash, baseball,
  cricket), yet competitive play with one eye is documented. (Artificial
  Eyes: <https://artificialeyes.net/adjusting-to-eye-loss-mind-map/eye-loss-sports/>;
  AAO Sports & Vision: <https://www.aao.org/museum/exhibition-detail/sports-vision>)
- Elite ball-sport performance does not require perfect stereopsis;
  compensation likely via looming and relative size — see [barrett-2017-elite-athletes](../../sources/barrett-2017-elite-athletes/).
- Protection is mandatory for functionally one-eyed athletes — see
  [eye-protection](../eye-protection/).

## Implications for the project

- A sports-and-hobbies guide with realistic, encouraging framing and a hard
  cross-link to the protection guide.

## Open questions

- Dynamic visual acuity / motion-in-depth differences specific to uniocular
  athletes are not well characterized.
