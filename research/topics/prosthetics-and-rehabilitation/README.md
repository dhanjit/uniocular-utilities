# Topic: Prosthetics and rehabilitation

For people who have lost an eye entirely, rehabilitation is partly prosthetic
and strongly psychosocial.

## Summary

Early prosthesis fitting improves quality of life and is anatomically (not
just cosmetically) important; structured rehabilitation programs exist but
are underused.

## Key findings

- Early prosthesis fitting improves QoL (conformer ~2 months
  post-enucleation; prosthesis within ~3 months of the conformer). (Case
  series: <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5379827/>)
- Longitudinal prosthesis trial: gains in general health/bodily pain, but
  not global stress — see [makrakis-2021-prosthesis-qol](../../sources/makrakis-2021-prosthesis-qol/).
- A prosthesis is anatomically important (eyelid form, lacrimal flux,
  orbicularis tone) and un-rehabilitated anophthalmia can drive social
  avoidance — Goiato et al. (editorial), *Expert Review of Ophthalmology*
  2018: <https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17469899.2018.1503534>.
- A multidisciplinary monocular rehabilitation protocol exists — see
  [ihrig-amvr](../../sources/ihrig-amvr/).

## Implications for the project

- "First 90 days" manual: what prosthetic rehab involves and its
  psychosocial dimension; person-first language.
