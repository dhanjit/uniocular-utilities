# Topic: Field of view and the blind side

Uniocular vision loses the temporal crescent on the affected side, creating
a blind side where hazards go undetected. Hemianopia research transfers
usefully.

## Summary

Two mitigation routes: behavioral (deliberate blind-side scanning) and
technological (prism optics today; AR/electronic aids emerging).

## Key findings

- A large blind-side **scan** helps detect hazards; a minimum scan magnitude
  can be a training/AT target — see [driving-hemianopia-vii-2021](../../sources/driving-hemianopia-vii-2021/).
- **Multiplexing prisms** expand the field ~20–43° without an apical scotoma,
  with a dedicated monocular trial — see [peli-2017-multiplexing-prism](../../sources/peli-2017-multiplexing-prism/).
- Electronic **collision-warning** devices warn of mid/high obstacles a cane
  would miss. (<https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT03057496>)
- AR overlays can increase the effective field of view. (MDPI Vision:
  <https://www.mdpi.com/2411-5150/6/4/67>)

## Implications for the project

- Justifies the **blind-side-assist** utility (collision warning, scan
  prompts) and the **alerting** module.
- Prism optics are reference material, not something the project builds.

## Open questions

- Haptic/spatial-audio sensory-substitution tuned to the ~35° monocular blind
  side is largely unstudied (a flagged gap).
