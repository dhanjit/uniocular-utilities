# Adaptation to monocular vision (Kraut & Lopez-Fernandez, 2002)

**Citation:** Kraut JA, Lopez-Fernandez V. "Adaptation to monocular
vision." *International Ophthalmology Clinics*. 2002;42(3):203–213.
**Identifiers:** PMID 12131596.
**Access:** Paywalled (abstract: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12131596/>;
journal citation: <https://journals.lww.com/internat-ophthalmology/citation/2002/07000/adaptation_to_monocular_vision.21.aspx>).
**Verification:** Journal, year, and the 8.8 vs. 3.6-month figure verified
via web search. The n=65 / 91%-untrained / 30-question survey specifics
come from the Gemini deep-research synthesis and are not independently
confirmed; a closely related paper (Coday & Warner, "Acquired Monocular
Vision: Functional Consequences From the Patient's Perspective", *Ophthalmic
Plast Reconstr Surg* 2002) covers similar ground.

## Scope

Retrospective survey of patients with acquired monocular vision (AMV) after
enucleation / phthisis bulbi, characterizing functional and psychosocial
impact and the adaptation timeline.

## Key points

- Sudden vision loss adapted in ~**8.8 months** on average vs. ~**3.6
  months** for gradual loss — implying the cortex re-weights monocular cues
  before total unilateral loss.
- Reported sequelae: depth-perception difficulty, cosmesis concerns, reading
  strain, and serious psychosocial harm (depression, job loss, alcoholism).
- ~**91% received no formal rehabilitation** (per synthesis).

## Learnings for this project

- Anchors the "first 90 days" / adaptation framing: expect months, and
  faster after gradual loss.
- The near-total absence of rehabilitation is the core unmet need this
  project's manuals address.
