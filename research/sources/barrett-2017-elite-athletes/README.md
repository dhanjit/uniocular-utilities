# Vision and visual history in elite athletes (Barrett et al., 2017)

**Citation:** Barrett BT, et al. "Vision and Visual History in
Elite/Near-Elite-Level Cricketers and Rugby-League Players." *Sports
Medicine - Open*. 2017;3:39.
**Identifiers:** PMID 29127516; DOI 10.1186/s40798-017-0106-z.
**Access:** Open access (<https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5681452/>).
**Verification:** Verified. Journal corrected from an earlier mis-citation
("Optometry and Vision Science"); on-field-issue figure confirmed at ~27%.

## Scope

Cross-sectional measurement of acuity, colour vision, and TNO near
stereoacuity in 59 high-level athletes (16 elite female cricketers, 23
near-elite male cricketers, 20 rugby-league players).

## Key points

- ~**27%** had sub-optimal habitual on-field vision, mostly **uncorrected
  refractive error**.
- Only **elite cricketers** exceeded stereoacuity norms (p < 0.001);
  near-elite cricketers and rugby players did not.
- Implies peak binocular stereopsis is **not** a strict prerequisite for
  elite dynamic interception.

## Learnings for this project

- Evidence for the **sports** guide: absent perfect stereopsis does not
  preclude high-level ball-sport performance; compensation likely via
  *looming* and relative size.
- Supports an encouraging, realistic tone rather than a deficit framing.
