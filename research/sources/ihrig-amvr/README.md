# Acquired Monocular Vision Rehabilitation program (Ihrig)

**Citation:** Ihrig C. "Acquired Monocular Vision Rehabilitation program."
*Journal of Rehabilitation Research & Development* (VA), 2007;44(4). A
related write-up is indexed as Ihrig, "Vision Rehabilitation Team Management
of Acquired Monocular Vision," ~2013.
**Access:** Open (VA): <https://www.rehab.research.va.gov/jour/07/44/4/ihrig.html>.
Related: <https://www.researchgate.net/publication/235522108>.
**Verification:** VA write-up is openly accessible; treat the protocol
description as authoritative, the single-case outcomes as anecdotal.

## Scope

Describes the multidisciplinary **Acquired Monocular Vision Rehabilitation
(AMVR)** protocol (developed at a VA medical center): low-vision optometry,
orientation & mobility (O&M), blind-rehabilitation specialists, and
psychosocial counseling tailored to losing one eye.

## Key points

- A structured rehabilitation pathway specifically for monocular (not
  low-vision/blind) patients exists.
- Reported single-patient outcomes: increased confidence, better near-space
  motor skill, reduced depression; shift from "disabled" to "functional"
  self-perception.

## Learnings for this project

- A template for what good monocular rehabilitation contains — directly
  shapes the manual structure.
- Notes that low-vision programs (eccentric viewing, cane travel) are
  **inadequate** for a uniocular patient with good central acuity but a
  temporal field defect.
