# Multiplexing prisms for field expansion (Peli, 2017)

**Citation:** Peli E, et al. "Multiplexing Prisms for Field Expansion."
*Optometry and Vision Science*. 2017.
**Identifiers:** PMID 28727615.
**Access:** Open access (<https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5542858/>).
**Related trial:** "Multiplexing Prism Fitting for Field Expansion of
Monocular Vision" — <https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT06027216>.
**Verification:** Verified; field-expansion figures corrected to 20–43°.

## Scope

Describes the multiplexing prism (MxP): a Fresnel-like device with
alternating prism and flat elements that superimposes a shifted, see-through
view to expand the visual field **without an apical scotoma** (the central
blind spot that makes traditional Fresnel prisms hazardous for mobility).

## Key points

- Field expansion of ~**20–43°** depending on design (earlier designs
  20–30°; newer up to 36° and 43°), with added scanning range.
- Eliminating the apical scotoma is the key advance for safe walking/driving.
- Half or more of fitted patients keep using the device long-term and report
  improved mobility.

## Learnings for this project

- State-of-the-art **optical** blind-side mitigation; reference point for any
  software/AR blind-side-assist utility.
- A dedicated **monocular** field-expansion trial (NCT06027216) signals
  clinical interest specific to our population.
