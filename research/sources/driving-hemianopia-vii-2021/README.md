# Driving with hemianopia VII: gaze and head scan magnitude (2021)

**Citation:** "Driving With Hemianopia VII: Predicting Hazard Detection With
Gaze and Head Scan Magnitude." 2021.
**Identifiers:** PMID 33510959.
**Access:** Open access (<https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7804568/>).
**Verification:** From search digest.

## Scope

Studies how the magnitude of blind-side gaze/head scanning predicts hazard
detection in drivers with homonymous hemianopia — a field-loss profile that
overlaps functionally with uniocular blind-side loss.

## Key points

- A large **blind-side scan** helps quickly identify hazards.
- A **minimum scan magnitude** can serve as a training criterion or as a
  target metric for assistive technology.

## Learnings for this project

- Justifies scan-training guidance in manuals and a scan-prompt feature in
  the **blind-side-assist** utility.
- Hemianopia research is a useful (partial) transfer source for uniocular
  field loss.
