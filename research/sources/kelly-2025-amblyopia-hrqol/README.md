# Functional consequences of amblyopia and HRQoL (Kelly et al., 2025)

**Citation:** Kelly KR, et al. "Functional consequences of amblyopia and its
impact on health-related quality of life." *Vision Research*. 2025.
**Identifiers:** PMID 40319624; ScienceDirect S0042698925000732.
**Access:** Paywalled (abstract: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40319624/>;
<https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0042698925000732>).
**Verification:** PMID exists; quantitative details (n=114 cohorts, ~122.7
wpm) are from the Gemini synthesis and the search digest, not independently
confirmed.

## Scope

Combines visuo-cognitive tests, motor evaluations, and quality-of-life
scores across amblyopic, strabismic, and control adults (reported n≈114:
23 pure amblyopia, 52 amblyopia+strabismus, 20 strabismus, 19 controls).

## Key points

- Amblyopia interferes with contrast sensitivity, eye–hand coordination,
  and reading mechanics (reported ~**122.7 wpm**, below binocular norms).
- Psychosocial impact scores were notably depressed vs. controls.

## Learnings for this project

- Caution mapping to *acquired* monocularity: amblyopia involves cortical
  suppression / developmental deficit, whereas an enucleated adult retains a
  developed cortex and fatigues mainly from monocular accommodation and
  scanning. End-points (reading fatigue, slower fine motor, reduced QoL)
  nonetheless converge.
