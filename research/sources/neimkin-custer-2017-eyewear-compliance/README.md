# Protective lens wear compliance in anophthalmic patients (Neimkin & Custer, 2017)

**Citation:** Neimkin MG, Custer PL. "Compliance With Protective Lens Wear in
Anophthalmic Patients." *Ophthalmic Plastic and Reconstructive Surgery*.
2017;33(1):61–64.
**Identifiers:** PMID 26866334.
**Access:** Open access (<https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4980283/>).
**Verification:** Verified — journal, year, n=132, and etiology breakdown
confirmed via web search.

## Scope

Retrospective chart review of 132 anophthalmic patients (one remaining
sighted eye), evaluating how often they wear protective lenses and what
predicts compliance. Etiologies: trauma 33.3%, blind painful eye 33.3%,
congenital 14.4%, malignancy 14.4%, retinoblastoma 4.5%.

## Key points

- Prior glasses-wearers complied ~**79.7%** of the time; ~**67.1%** of prior
  non-wearers were **non-compliant**.
- Non-compliance reasons: "lack of concern" (**43%**), aesthetics (**26%**),
  felt unnecessary because acuity already sharp (**17%**).
- Compliance correlated **significantly with the number of clinical
  encounters** — repeated structured counseling is the main proven lever.

## Learnings for this project

- The highest-yield safety message (protect the remaining eye) faces a
  *behavioral*, not informational, barrier.
- Any reminder/habit tooling should lean on repeated reinforcement and take
  aesthetic objections seriously.
