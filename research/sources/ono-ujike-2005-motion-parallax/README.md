# Motion parallax driven by head movements (Ono & Ujike, 2005)

**Citation:** Ono H, Ujike H. "Motion parallax driven by head movements:
conditions for visual stability, perceived depth, and perceived concomitant
motion." *Perception*. 2005;34(4). (Some databases index this under *Vision
Research*.)
**Identifiers:** PMID 15943054.
**Access:** Paywalled (abstract: <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15943054/>).
**Verification:** PMID is stable; journal of record not independently
re-verified (synthesis lists *Perception*).

## Scope

Three psychophysics experiments yoking a visual stimulus to the observer's
lateral head movement, mapping when motion parallax yields stable perceived
depth vs. an illusion of object motion.

## Key points

- There is a **"stability zone"** where depth is perceived without
  concomitant motion (location constancy).
- **Slower head movements require greater parallax magnitude** (higher
  retinal-image-velocity to head-velocity ratio) for equivalent perceived
  depth.
- Beyond threshold, the percept breaks into concomitant motion (the object
  appears to move with the head).

## Learnings for this project

- Directly informs the **depth-cue trainer**: parallax exercises must tune
  stimulus motion to head-movement speed to stay in the stability zone.
- Tested patched binocular subjects on CRTs — long-term monocular adapters
  may differ, a noted gap.
