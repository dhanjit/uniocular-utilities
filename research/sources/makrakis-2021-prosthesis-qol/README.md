# Ocular prosthesis impact on QoL (Makrakis et al., 2021)

**Citation:** Makrakis LR, et al. "The Impact of an Ocular Prosthesis on the
Quality of Life, Perceived Stress, and Clinical Adaptation of Anophthalmic
Patients: A Clinical and Longitudinal Trial." *Journal of Prosthodontics*.
2021.
**Identifiers:** DOI 10.1111/jopr.13332.
**Access:** Paywalled (<https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jopr.13332>).
**Verification:** Details from the Gemini synthesis; not independently
confirmed beyond the citation.

## Scope

Prospective longitudinal trial using SF-36 and PSS-10 in 26 anophthalmic
patients, evaluated pre-installation and at 3 and 6 months after fitting an
ocular prosthesis.

## Key points

- Significant gains in SF-36 **"Bodily Pain"** and **"General Health"**.
- **No** significant reduction in global perceived stress (PSS-10).
- Chronic localized discharge common; no severe socket inflammation.

## Learnings for this project

- Informs the prosthetics/rehabilitation content: a prosthesis helps general
  health perception but does not by itself resolve stress — psychosocial
  support remains necessary.
- Generic instruments (SF-36/PSS-10) may miss cosmesis/eye-contact effects.
