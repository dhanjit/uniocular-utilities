# Asthenopia: systematic review and meta-analysis (2026)

**Citation:** "Clinical manifestations, prevalence, and risk factors of
asthenopia: a systematic review and meta-analysis." *Journal of Global
Health*. 2026;16:04053.
**Access:** Open access (<https://jogh.org/2026/jogh-16-04053/>;
<https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12879263/>).
**Verification:** From search digest.

## Scope

Systematic review and meta-analysis of asthenopia (eye strain) prevalence,
symptoms, and risk factors.

## Key points

- Pooled prevalence: eye tiredness ~**65%**, strain/fatigue ~**47%**.
- Driven by sustained accommodation/convergence during near work; **unequal
  refractive error between the eyes** is itself a contributor.
- The **20–20–20 rule** and structured breaks are supported mitigations.

## Learnings for this project

- Baseline for the **eye-strain-manager** utility (break timing, contrast,
  text size) and the screens/computing manual.
- Note: this is general asthenopia, not uniocular-specific — a flagged gap.
