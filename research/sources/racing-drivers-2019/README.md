# Implications of monocular vision for racing drivers (2019)

**Citation:** "Implications of monocular vision for racing drivers." *PLOS
ONE*. 2019;14(12):e0226308.
**Identifiers:** PMID 31841526; DOI 10.1371/journal.pone.0226308.
**Access:** Open access
(<https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0226308>;
<https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6913915/>).
**Verification:** Figures from search digest of the open-access article.

## Scope

Driving-simulator study of racing drivers comparing binocular baseline with
a sudden monocular condition and a sudden monocular *reduction* condition.

## Key points

- Under **sudden monocular** condition: drivers were **2.1–6.5×** more likely
  to collide; reaction time rose by ~**64–126 ms**.
- A sudden monocular **reduction** in vision **with the peripheral field
  preserved did not** impair performance — the field loss / monocularity
  itself is the issue, not reduced acuity per se.

## Learnings for this project

- Key nuance for the **driving** guide: it is the *suddenness* and the *field
  loss* that hurt, not monocularity once adapted.
- Distinguishes "newly monocular" risk from "established monocular" capability.
