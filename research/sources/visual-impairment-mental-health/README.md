# Visual impairment and mental health (review)

**Citation:** "Visual Impairment and Mental Health: Unmet Needs and Treatment
Options." (See also "Psychiatric disorders linked to visual impairment".)
**Access:** Open access (<https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7721280/>;
<https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12836230/>).
**Verification:** From search digest.

## Scope

Reviews the relationship between visual impairment and mental-health
outcomes and treatment options.

## Key points

- Adults with visual impairment are roughly **2×** as likely to experience
  depression; ~**1 in 4** adults with vision loss report anxiety or
  depression (CDC).
- The relationship is **bidirectional** — stress/anxiety can worsen perceived
  vision.
- **Acceptance and social support** improve adjustment to irreversible loss.

## Learnings for this project

- Mandates a frank, supportive emotional-adjustment section in the "first 90
  days" manual and signposting to mental-health resources.
- Sets a respectful, non-deficit tone across all content.
