# Research

Curated research underpinning the uniocular-utilities project — what is
known about monocular vision, depth perception, adaptation, and assistive
technology, with citations and links to the sources.

## Contents

- **[literature-review.md](literature-review.md)** — a deep-dive annotated
  bibliography across 15 topic areas (epidemiology, adaptation, depth cues,
  stereopsis loss, driving, field loss, eye protection, eye strain, sports,
  children, mental health, prosthetics, AR/AI assistive tech, and
  standards), each with key findings, source links, and ties to the
  project's [roadmap](../ROADMAP.md). Ends with a research-gaps section.
- **[deep-research-synthesis.md](deep-research-synthesis.md)** — an
  external AI-generated deep-research report (Gemini), reproduced for
  transparency with a verification-status table. Treated as provisional;
  only spot-verified findings are folded into the curated review above.

## Why this is here

The project's [guiding principles](../ROADMAP.md#guiding-principles)
require that advice be evidence-based. This directory is the evidence
base: before a manual gives guidance or a utility makes a design choice,
it should be traceable to research recorded here.

## Methodology and limitations

- This is a **curated first pass**, not a systematic review. Entries were
  gathered from web and database searches and are summarized in good
  faith.
- Many primary sources are **paywalled**. Where possible, entries link to
  open-access mirrors (PubMed Central, journal open-access pages). We do
  **not** redistribute copyrighted PDFs in this repository; we link to
  them.
- Findings are summarized from abstracts and open-access text. Anyone
  building on a finding **should read the primary source** and verify it.
- This collection is informational. It is **not medical advice** and does
  not replace a clinician.

## Contributing

To add research: cite it in `literature-review.md` under the right topic,
summarize the key finding in one or two sentences, link an open-access
source where one exists, and note which roadmap item it informs. See
[CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md).
